Tips on self medication in between injections

Change position often Hot and cold therapy Exercise and eat a balanced diet Over the counter pain medication

Change position often

When writing or driving, release your grip every 10 to 15 minutes. When watching television or using the computer, get up and move around every 30 minutes. Its important to not overwork your joints or not move thme for an extended period of time in order to reduce the chance of a flare up and to avoid tighness. Stretching your joints often also provides relief.

Hot and cold therapy

You can use both types of temperature therapy depending on your specific symptoms. Heat can be used to warm up stiff joints that have not been active recently to loosen them up. Ice packs can be useful for inflammed areas to provide pain relief to that specific area.

Exercise and eat a balanced diet

Diet and exercise is important for everybody regardless if you're a person with rheumatoid arthritis. A healthly diet provides the body with the correct nutrients and proteins it needs to function as best as it can. Exercise in moderation can help your joints stay active and cause less stiffness. A common recommendation that doctors tell their patients is swimming because your entire body is active while beign under less stress from the water.

Over the counter pain medication

Medicines like motrin, advil, tylenol, etc provide temporary pain relief to affected areas. These are not long term solutions but in times of need relieve pain to all areas for a few hours.
